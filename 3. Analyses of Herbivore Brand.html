Question¶

What about Herbivore products is resonating with consumers and has this changed over time?¶

I will use topic modeling for Herbivore alone for a subset of Herbivore's products that were given a rating of 5 by the consumer. I've chosen this subset of data because if something about the products is resonating with the consumers, then it must mean that the consumers are happy with the products or that they love/like something about those products - therefore the consumers are most likely to give very high ratings to these products.

# This function preprocesses text data 

def process_words(texts, stop_words=stop_words, allowed_postags=['NOUN', 'ADJ', 'VERB', 'ADV']):
    """Remove stopwords, form Bigrams, trigrams and lemmatization"""
    texts = [[word for word in simple_preprocess(str(doc)) if word not in stop_words] for doc in texts]
    texts = [bigram_mod[doc] for doc in texts]
    texts = [trigram_mod[bigram_mod[doc]] for doc in texts]
    texts_out = []
    nlp = spacy.load('en', disable=['parser', 'ner'])
    for sent in texts:
        doc = nlp(" ".join(sent)) 
        texts_out.append([token.lemma_ for token in doc if token.pos_ in allowed_postags])
    # remove stopwords once more after lemmatization
    texts_out = [[word for word in simple_preprocess(str(doc)) if word not in stop_words] for doc in texts_out]    
    return texts_out

def format_topics_sentences(ldamodel=None, corpus=corpus, texts='Herbivore'):
    # Init output
    sent_topics_df = pd.DataFrame()

    # Get main topic in each document
    for i, row_list in enumerate(ldamodel[corpus]):
        row = row_list[0] if ldamodel.per_word_topics else row_list            
        # print(row)
        row = sorted(row, key=lambda x: (x[1]), reverse=True)
        # Get the Dominant topic, Perc Contribution and Keywords for each document
        for j, (topic_num, prop_topic) in enumerate(row):
            if j == 0:  # => dominant topic
                wp = ldamodel.show_topic(topic_num)
                topic_keywords = ", ".join([word for word, prop in wp])
                sent_topics_df = sent_topics_df.append(pd.Series([int(topic_num), round(prop_topic,4), topic_keywords]), ignore_index=True)
            else:
                break
    sent_topics_df.columns = ['Dominant_Topic', 'Perc_Contribution', 'Topic_Keywords']

    # Add original text to the end of the output
    contents = pd.Series(texts)
    sent_topics_df = pd.concat([sent_topics_df, contents], axis=1)
    return(sent_topics_df)

Something interesting is happening here.

OBSERVATION 1 There is some seasonality apparent here: generally, the review volumes spike up every year around the major winter holiday season (month 12). More minor spikes are also noticable in mid-summer months (month 7). Unfortunately this data is not ample enough to conclude with certainty that there is in fact seasonality in review volumes, but it's a good starting point to invstigate further. If I had more time, I would make this same plot for some other longer established brand which has a lot more data and then repeat the process for a few other brands to see if the same pattern arises. But due to time constraints I'll skip testing this to confirm the seasonality pattern.

Assumptions regarding observation 1 Let's assume that this observed pattern is in fact seasonality in the data. The pattern would indicate the volume of reviews goes up around winter holiday season and midsummer. Another assumption I'm making here is that there must be some positive correlation between volume of sales and the volume of reviews consumers posts. This correlation might be weak, but I presume it would nevertheless be there. It's expected that sales would go up around winter holiday season given that consumers are shopping for gifts for their friends and family. It's also expected that, especially in the beauty products industry, sales would go up around mid summer months too - because of the warmer whether and a correlation that exhists between summer season and vacationing, trips to the beach, bikini season, etc. Summer is probably one of the more profit generating seasons for beauty-product retailers with increased sales not just due to vacation season but also due to increased media marketing on the necessity of sunscreens, self-tanners, beach wavy hair sprays, bright colored makeup trends, etc...So then, if the seasonality ovserved here in review volumes can be explained by expected seasonality in sales, then we may conclude that the volume of reviews might be a reasonably good proxy variable for sales. In the absence of sales data, then we might be able to use review volume to gain insights about the overall performance of the brand.

OBSERVATION 2 There is an anomalously large looking spike in the volume of reviews in month 12, 2015. Conclusions cannot be made without further information as to why the review volume spiked up so high during this year, but here are a couple of things I assume might be a reasonable cause for this:

  1. The data for Herbivore brand starts a few months before this anomalous spike. Maybe there was no data before this date because it's around this date that the brand was launched. If that's the case, the spike about the brand's first holiday season might be explained by the brand's deliberate grand-opening marketing and promotional efforts. Further research is needed to confirm or refute this assumption.

  2. The spike might be due to a time where a major beauty product retailer such as Sephora picked up the brand and launched a marketing campaign for it in their stores. Given that the data we're dealing with here comes from Sephora, I would assume this is more likely than point 1 stated above. Sephora might have picked up Herbivore at around month 9, 2015 which is why we might have our data starting at that time. Then Sephora may have taken a couple of months to plan and implement a marketing strategy for the brand, much like they do for all new brands they acquire and carry in their stores or when they wish to refresh the consumer's awareness of a brand that has been going a bit stale at their stores. If this is true, then that would also be potential cause for the spike in review volumes around this time. Again, without further research I cannot confirm this assumption.

OBSERVATION 3 The longer term trend of review volumes seems to be growing slowly and weakly, but growing nevertheless. What's most interesting is the faster growth in review volumes in 2018. The mid-summer spike seems significantly larger than in previous years, and if the pattenr were to continue throuugh month 12 2018, we would expect to see a much larger spike in the winter holiday season too (this is speculative extrapolation of the data). Overall, it looks like consumer engagement via review counts (and perhaps sales) were growing more quickly in 2018 than they did in the previous years. Either something changed in 2018 (perhaps CircleUp partnered with the brand??) or the effects of consumers becoming more familiar with the brand over time are really starting to kick-in here. In any case, the brand's overall performance seems to be improving over time.

This can also be backed by the additional insight when looking at the 365 day moving averages of Herbivore product review upvotes/downvotes plot above. While the overall trend is growing over time, there is a particularly noticable spike in consumers who are reading and upvoting/downvoting reviews around the mid-summer month. This coincides with OBSERVATION 3.
